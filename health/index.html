05

Immediate actions & solutions

Do these

Ordered by priority. The first two are appointments to book; the rest are things you can start now and confirm with your doctor.

1
Book a diabetes evaluation This week
Two HbA1c readings at 6.7 (Nov & Jul), both in the diabetic range, plus fasting glucose at 122 — this meets the threshold for a formal work-up rather than watchful waiting.
Solution
  • See a physician or endocrinologist and bring all three reports so they can see the full trend.
  • Expect a confirmatory repeat HbA1c + fasting glucose; the diagnosis is made on repeat testing.
  • Ask specifically whether you're pre-diabetic or diabetic, and whether lifestyle alone is enough or medication (e.g. metformin) is worth discussing — that's a doctor's call, not a self-decision.
2
Get an abdominal ultrasound Soon
ALT (55.8) and AST (40.2) are both above range and rising, alongside high sugar and cholesterol — the classic fingerprint of non-alcoholic fatty liver.
Solution
  • Ask your doctor to order a simple, non-invasive liver ultrasound to check for fatty change.
  • Mention the rising enzyme trend and the mildly high direct bilirubin so they have the context.
  • The good news: early fatty liver is reversible with the same weight, diet and glucose steps below.
3
Start the metabolic lifestyle plan Start now
Glucose, LDL, HDL and liver enzymes share one root — insulin resistance. The same levers move all four at once, and your April numbers prove they respond in your body.
Solution — evidence-based basics to confirm with your doctor
  • Cut refined carbs & added sugar (white rice, sweets, sugary drinks, refined flour) — the biggest lever on both HbA1c and triglycerides.
  • Move ~150 min/week — brisk walking counts; a 10–15 min walk after meals blunts glucose spikes noticeably.
  • Strength train 2×/week — building muscle directly improves how your body handles insulin.
  • More fibre, less saturated fat — vegetables, whole grains, legumes for glucose & LDL; swap fried/processed fats.
  • Aim for gradual, steady change rather than crash dieting — sustainable habits are what held in April.
4
Raise the lipids & ESR at that same visit Discuss
LDL 136.8 with HDL at 35.6 compounds cardiovascular risk on top of the glucose picture; ESR has stayed elevated (24–25) across all three tests without ever normalising.
Solution
  • Discuss LDL management — whether the lifestyle plan is sufficient or a statin is warranted given your combined risk profile.
  • Ask about adding a CRP test to help interpret the persistent ESR — it's non-specific on its own and worth a closer look.
  • Re-test the full panel in ~3 months to confirm the plan is working, matching your existing testing rhythm.
